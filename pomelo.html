The Pomelo

The giant of the citrus world with a remarkably mild flavor.

What is a Pomelo?

The pomelo (Citrus maxima) is the largest naturally occurring citrus fruit on Earth. Native to Southeast Asia, it features a characteristic thick, pillowy rind and large, crisp juice sacs that separate easily from one another without bursting.

Key Characteristics

  • Taste: Sweet and mild, completely lacking the sharp bitterness of its descendant, the grapefruit.
  • Peel: Extremely thick, soft, and spongy, ranging from pale green to soft yellow.
  • Ancestry: A principal ancestral citrus species that was crossed with sweet orange to create the grapefruit.

Popular Varieties

Pomelos are grown widely under different names:

  • Honey Pomelo: A highly popular variety with pale yellow skin and sweet, juicy flesh.
  • Tambun Pomelo: A famous Malaysian variety known for its exceptional sweetness and thin rind.
  • Siam Sweet: A classic Thai variety that boasts a completely acidless, sweet flavor profile.

Cultural Significance

In Southeast Asian and Chinese culture, pomelos are prominent symbols of prosperity, health, and family unity. They are a staple centerpiece during the Mid-Autumn Festival and Lunar New Year, where the outer skin is sometimes carved into festive hats for children.
